Grilled Pork Chops

This is a regular go-to for me. It's quick, easy and doesn't cost much.

Ingredients

  • Thin-cut bone in pork chops
  • Cayenne Pepper to taste
  • Ground black pepper
  • Low-sodium soy sauce
  • Garlic, minced, or to taste

Directions

  1. Season pork chops, lightly poke with fork to make a few holes. Marinate in soy-sauce and garlic.
  2. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil grate.
  3. Remove chops from marinade and shake off excess. Discard remaining marinade.
  4. Place chops on preheated grill and reduce heat to medium. Cook until no longer pink in center, about 6 minutes per side.

Coconut Curry With Shrimp

This is a great one when you want a little spice in your life.

Ingredients

  • Vegetable Oil
  • White Onion
  • Red Bell Pepper
  • Jalapeno
  • Unsweetened Coconut Milk

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a large pan
  2. Reduce heat, add coconut milk, curry paste, brown sugar, and fish sauce

  4. Pat shrimp dry and add to sauce along with cooked veggies. Allow curry to simmer gently until shrimp are cooked.

Crepes

Breakfast is the most important meal of the day, and crepes are a great way to start it off!

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Water
  • Butter

Directions

  1. Mix flour and eggs, gradually adding milk and water.
  2. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium high heat. pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each crepe.
  3. Cook the crepe for about 2 minutes, until the bottom is light brown. Loosen with a spatula, turn and cook the other side. Serve hot.
